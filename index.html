About the Studio

Disem Art operates as an indie studio shaped by research, observation, and methodical breakdown. Each body of work begins with close study, understanding how something is built before deciding how it should be represented. The studio values patience over speed and depth over surface, treating the creative process as an act of inquiry, rather than mere visual expression.

Teardown Artwork Care & Handling Guide

Each teardown artwork is an assembled study composed of multiple elements, carefully arranged and housed within a shadow box frame. Due to the dimensional and material-specific nature of these works, mindful handling and display are essential. Here is how you should take care of it.

Handling

Always handle the artwork by the frame. Avoid opening the shadow box or touching the internal components, as these elements are fragile and secured with minimal grip.

Display

Display indoors in a stable environment. Avoid placing the artwork in direct sunlight or near heat, humidity, air vents, kitchens, bathrooms, or areas with rapid vibrations and temperature changes. These artworks are intended to be securely and levelly wall-mounted on low-traffic areas. Placement on tables, desks, or other active surfaces is not recommended.

Cleaning & Maintenance

Do not use liquids, sprays, or chemical cleaners on the artwork or frame. To remove dust, gently wipe the exterior using a clean, dry microfiber cloth.

Aging & Material Character

Some artworks may include organic or antique components that can develop subtle changes over time. These variations are considered part of the work’s material character and should not be treated as defects.

With proper care, your teardown artwork will remain a preserved study of structure, function, and form.
